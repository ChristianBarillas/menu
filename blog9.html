Round 2: Classes by groups 1 & 2

Hello everyone, welcome once more to my English Didactics II blog! üòÅ

We've embarked on our third and final segment and delved into round 2 of our classes.

The first team introduced fresh vocabulary related to daily routines to the students. They encouraged the students to craft short paragraphs detailing their daily activities using the new words and adverbs of frequency. The students enthusiastically echoed the sentences and engaged in group activities to match sentences on the board. To make things more engaging, they handed out materials and gave the students around 2 minutes to work on them while some background music played, following the principles of Suggestopedia. It was evident that all the students were completely absorbed, working diligently to grasp the new vocabulary. The lesson was exceptionally well-structured, with each team member presenting engaging activities to facilitate student learning.

The second group's focus was on teaching grammar, and they did an outstanding job! We all know that grammar can be a daunting challenge from a student's perspective, and it's twice as demanding for teachers to convey it effectively. However, Maru's team conducted an interactive session that left a lasting impression. To kick things off, they had all the students stand up and initiated a memory chain game. Each student had to recall the daily routine of the preceding student and then share their own. It was a highly enjoyable and interactive approach to learning. Kudos to them for a job well done."
