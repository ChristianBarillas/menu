Teaching writing

Greetings once again! Welcome back! Today's topic will delve into a more theoretical aspect compared to our previous entries: Teaching Writing.

When we refer to writing skills, we encompass a multitude of elements such as correct spelling, proper punctuation, legible handwriting, constructing well-formed sentences, organizing paragraphs effectively, ensuring coherence within a text, and using appropriate style and register. As a writing teacher, the primary role involves being an attentive observer, a valuable resource, and a provider of constructive feedback. At the start of our class, our instructor, Orlando, engaged us in an enjoyable activity of tracing and practicing different handwriting styles. We also explored various strategies that students can employ to enhance their writing abilities, including:

Engaging in reading or listening to music and podcasts to expose oneself to new vocabulary. Actively experimenting with writing through regular practice. Incorporating newly acquired vocabulary into both spoken and written language. Regarding student writing, different approaches can be adopted, and sometimes the emphasis lies on the writing process itself. For instance, adhering to the widely known "writing process" entails pre-writing, drafting, revising, proofreading, and editing stages, leading to the final version of a piece of writing. During this journey, teachers primarily focus on guiding students through the process, offering feedback and support. In our class, we had the opportunity to craft a concise composition.

Lastly, Orlando introduced us to diverse methods for presenting vocabulary, or lexis. These methods include employing word charts where students match word segments to construct sentences, employing diagrams featuring related words, utilizing images to label objects, utilizing lists and glossaries, and employing topic webs.
