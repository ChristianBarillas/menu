Grammar and vocabulary!

Hey, welcome back to my blog! Greetings and welcome to a fresh blog post. Today, we took a unique approach where students assumed the roles of educators, resulting in a truly enriching experience. This time around, we broke away from the usual routine by dividing the students into four groups. Each group was tasked with teaching various aspects of grammar and vocabulary to their peers. The first group kicked off the session with a vocabulary lesson focused on medical terminology. They began by outlining the specific vocabulary they would cover and proceeded to present a slideshow featuring words along with their corresponding definitions. This approach ensured a comprehensive understanding of each term within its contextual framework. Furthermore, they handed out worksheets containing the explained words and definitions, challenging us to correctly match them. The lesson took an engaging turn as we participated in a lively game where we passed around a hat, danced briefly, and when the music ceased, the person holding the hat had to select a word and construct a sentence using it. To cap it off, we put our newfound vocabulary knowledge to the test by engaging in an online game that incorporated all the words we had learned during the session. It was a fantastic experience, with the first group delivering captivating activities. Following this engaging start, my group took the stage. Our task was to educate the class on a grammar topic, specifically the third conditional.
