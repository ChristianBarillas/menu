Classes by groups 5 & 6

In this week's educational journey, we explored the concepts of daily routines and the simple present tense, guided by the collective efforts of Group 3 and Group 4.

Greetings, dear readers! Let's dive into another enchanting update! ðŸ˜„ This week, the "Fabulous Five," affectionately known as Group 5, took center stage. They were the eager early birds, all prepared and ready to make the most of their class. Initially, a tinge of disappointment hung in the air as the class commenced with just one student, but as the session unfolded, more eager learners joined in, bringing a renewed sense of excitement.

Their journey began with an engaging icebreaker, the captivating "Human Knot" game, led by the skillful Miriam. It acted as the perfect catalyst to break the initial lull, infusing energy into a room that had started the day a tad lethargic. The game worked its magic, igniting smiles and enthusiasm among all participants. Following this lively start, the dynamic teacher, Fernando, took the reins to introduce the day's theme, centered around the art of telling time. His teaching style was nothing short of captivating, as he skillfully enriched the students' vocabulary. Then, it was time for the practice session, led by another dedicated member of the group. They brought along a whimsical "Magic Basket," filled with notes that held various daily activities. Each student drew a note at random, teamed up with a classmate, and inquired about the activity on their note, receiving a delightful response that revealed the time they typically engaged in it. This approach proved to be an enjoyable way to practice the topic and revisit the material from the previous week.

As the class sailed forward, Maria took the helm, orchestrating an interactive activity that demanded students to read and share the time for diverse cities. Her teaching was a masterclass in engagement, as she intertwined fascinating tidbits about cities and time. Teacher Nathaly then brought a touch of creativity, introducing handmade clocks for a hands-on practice session, followed by a spirited boys-versus-girls competition. The boys emerged victorious, infusing the class with a delightful sense of competitiveness. Who doesn't relish a friendly rivalry, after all? To bring the session to a harmonious close, Miriam wove a clever thread of camaraderie, devising an ingenious bingo game to recap everything that had been learned throughout the lesson. The first group to complete a row horizontally or vertically was hailed as the triumphant winner.

Shifting the spotlight to Group 6, they embarked on the significant topic of "There is and there are." This fundamental concept for early-stage learners seemed to have been beautifully grasped by all. The group executed a splendid teaching performance, with one student eloquently summarizing, "There is is used for one object, and there are is used for two or more things." It was heartwarming to witness this articulate explanation, a testament to the hard work and dedication of their classmates, who excelled in delivering lessons that resonated with everyone's understanding.

Particular admiration was reserved for Mauricio and Keneth, who radiated infectious enthusiasm throughout the class. Their energy and commitment to teaching shone brightly, making it clear that they genuinely reveled in the educational journey. The entire group contributed to a remarkable performance, unveiling a range of activities to help students practice counting and discerning when to use "Is" or "Are" while describing objects in pictures. Furthermore, they shared valuable insights about the structure of the past tense, "Was" and "Were," and even illuminated the difference in the Spanish equivalent, "Hay." This insight was instrumental in helping students bridge the gap between their native language and their second language understanding.
