The theme of the day was daily routines expressed through the lens of reading. We initiated our session by posing questions in Spanish, aimed at acquainting ourselves with the subject matter. This paved the way for an exploration of the vocabulary related to daily routines and the appropriate use of the simple present tense. We took a brief moment to emphasize the significance of the simple present tense in effectively describing essential and factual information. Subsequently, our class engaged in two captivating activities. The first activity entailed extracting questions related to daily routines from a reading material. We decided to pose these questions to the students first, instilling a sense of purpose in their reading. Afterward, they combed through the text to unearth the answers. In the second activity, our creative learners designed and shared their very own daily routines with the class. The class culminated with mixed feelings, as we bid farewell to a session we had grown so fond of: a touch of sadness lingering in our hearts. In summary, this week's classes were an enriching journey of language learning, with both Group 3 and Group 4 leaving indelible impressions on us.
